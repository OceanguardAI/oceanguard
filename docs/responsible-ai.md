# OceanGuard AI — Responsible AI Statement

---

## What OceanGuard Is

OceanGuard is a **decision-support tool** for marine conservation officers. It flags vessel detections near Marine Protected Areas for human review. It does not identify individuals, does not make enforcement decisions, and does not take any autonomous action.

The system is designed to reduce the burden on under-resourced conservation teams, not to replace their judgment.

---

## What OceanGuard Is Not

- **Not an enforcement system.** OceanGuard cannot and does not trigger any enforcement action. Every output is a recommendation for human review.
- **Not an accusation system.** Flagging a vessel as HIGH risk does not mean the vessel is engaged in illegal activity. It means the detection pattern warrants further investigation by a qualified officer.
- **Not infallible.** The model has a mAP50 of 0.838 on the HRSID validation set. False positives and false negatives exist. The system is transparent about this.
- **Not a surveillance system.** OceanGuard processes SAR satellite imagery of open ocean areas within or near MPAs. It does not track individuals, collect personal data, or integrate with any civilian surveillance infrastructure.

---

## The Human-in-the-Loop Principle

Every risk event generated by OceanGuard has a `review_status` field that starts at `"Pending"`. The valid states are:

- `Pending` — no human has reviewed this yet
- `Confirmed Risk` — a conservation officer has reviewed and assessed this as a genuine concern
- `False Positive` — a conservation officer has reviewed and dismissed this
- `Resolved` — action has been taken

**No event can move from Pending to Confirmed Risk without human action.** The system is architecturally incapable of autonomously confirming a risk.

---

## Architecture of the Decision

The risk score is produced by a **deterministic, auditable formula** — not a neural network. Every component of the score is traceable:

```
risk_score = (0.30 × effective_detection_confidence
            + 0.25 × AIS_non_match_score
            + 0.25 × MPA_proximity_score
            + 0.10 × fishing_activity_score
            + 0.10 × repeated_activity_score)
```

A conservation officer can look at any score and answer: "Why did this get a HIGH rating?" The answer is always a specific combination of the above factors, not a black-box neural network output.

Claude (the Anthropic AI model used in the agent layer) generates **explanations** of these scores in plain language. It does not generate the scores. The AI layer can be removed entirely without changing any risk assessment — the scores and levels are produced deterministically before the AI layer is ever called.

## Limitations and Uncertainty

### SAR Detection
- The YOLO11n model was trained on HRSID (multi-sensor SAR) and validated on xView3 (Sentinel-1). Real-world performance on other sensors or in different sea-state conditions may vary.
- Confidence threshold 0.45 is tuned for **recall** — the system prefers false alarms over missed detections. This means conservation officers will see some false positives.
- SAR detections cannot determine vessel type, flag, identity, or crew. A detection is only a "vessel-like object."

### AIS Non-Match
- AIS absence does not prove intent to evade. Causes include: equipment failure, poor signal propagation, fishing vessels under the voluntary reporting threshold, legitimate AIS exemptions.
- The AIS matching window (2 km / ±3 h) is a practical heuristic, not a legal standard.
- GFW AIS coverage is not global. In areas of sparse AIS coverage, `ais_data_available` is set to `false` and a neutral score (0.3) is assigned rather than treating absence as evidence.

### MPA Proximity
- MPA boundaries are from the WDPA database. Boundaries may not reflect the most current legal definitions.
- "Near MPA" (≤5 km) is a precautionary radius for patrol prioritisation, not a legal exclusion zone.

### Agent Layer
- Claude generates plain-language summaries based on structured event data. The agent layer does not have access to imagery, AIS raw feeds, or real-time data beyond what is passed to it.
- Agent outputs should be treated as analyst summaries, not legal opinions.

## Bias and Fairness

OceanGuard flags vessel detections based on objective signals (SAR detection, AIS presence/absence, MPA proximity). It does not incorporate vessel flag state, nationality, vessel type, or any demographic attribute.

The system cannot identify who is operating a vessel. It flags the spatial and temporal pattern of a detection — not the identity of any person.

---

## Data Privacy

OceanGuard processes:
- Satellite SAR imagery (public / licensed data, no personal data)
- AIS transponder broadcasts (public radio signals, vessel identifiers only)
- MPA boundary polygons (public geodata)
- Port location data (OpenStreetMap, public)

No personal data is collected, stored, or processed. No facial recognition, biometric data, or communication interception is involved.

---

## Transparency Commitments

1. **The risk formula is documented** in `docs/data-dictionary.md` and in the UI (Model Metrics page).
2. **Every score is traceable.** The evidence card shows every input factor that contributed to the score.
3. **Uncertainty is always stated.** The narrator agent is instructed to always produce an `uncertainty` field. The system never presents a result as certain.
4. **The system's limitations are disclosed** — in this document, in the UI footer, and in the evidence card.
5. **Model metrics are real and published** — mAP50 0.838, validation on xView3, 122 detections. No inflated numbers.

---

## Intended Use

OceanGuard is intended for use by:
- Marine protected area management authorities
- Marine law enforcement agencies with jurisdictional authority
- Conservation NGOs providing technical support to such authorities
- Researchers studying IUU fishing detection methods

It is not intended for use by private actors, commercial entities, or any party without conservation or enforcement authority over the relevant MPA.
